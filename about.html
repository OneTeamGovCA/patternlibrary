About the OneTeamGov Canada Pattern Library

Description

Accessibility statements are a way for Government of Canada organizations to describe how they're working to meet accessibility requirements, and what work still needs to be completed

Advantages

Accessibility statements make it easier for persons with disabilities to understand:

  • How they can best use a website or mobile application
  • Whether or not a website or mobile application is 'fully', 'partially,' or 'not' compliant with accessibility standards
  • How they can get alternatives to content that is not accessible to them
  • Who to contact to report an accessibility issue or ask for help

Examples in action

  • Accessibility Report developed by the Canadian Digital Service to accompany their statement in the now-retired COVID Alert application
  • Generate an accessibility statement - in English only

The OneTeamGov Canada Pattern Library is a community

The OneTeamGov Canada Pattern Library is a collaboration between the Governments of Canada, Alberta, British Columbia, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia and the Yukon Territory. Together, we represent a community of public servants committed to supporting government. Share your thoughts and suggestions with us by e-mail servicedigital-servicesnumerique@tbs-sct.gc.ca.
